I investigate complex threats across the software supply chain, malware, and adversary infrastructure, including cybercrime and APT activity, and translate technical findings into actionable intelligence and detection content. My work includes reverse-engineering malicious open source packages and extensions across major ecosystems (npm, PyPI, Go Modules, RubyGems, Maven, Crates, NuGet) and extension markets (Chrome and VS Code/Open VSX), tracking adversary infrastructure and campaigns, and developing detection logic for threat hunting. Previously, on Recorded Future's Insikt Group ARMOR team (Advanced Reversing, Malware, Operations, and Reconnaissance), I analyzed emerging malware and adversary tradecraft and authored YARA, Sigma, and Snort rules. Earlier roles at the United Nations, the American Bar Association, and the International Labor Rights Forum centered on investigations and programs combating trafficking, corruption, and labor abuses.
